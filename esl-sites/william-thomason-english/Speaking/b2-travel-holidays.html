PART 1 — Interview

Part 1: Interview

The examiner asks questions about everyday topics. Candidates answer individually.

Format

Duration: 2 minutes (pair) / 3 minutes (group of 3)

Focus: Giving personal information, expressing opinions on familiar topics

Topics covered:

  • Holiday destinations and preferences
  • Travel experiences and transport
  • Booking accommodation and planning trips
  • Tourist attractions and sightseeing
  • Travel abroad vs holidays at home

Candidates should give full answers — not just "yes" or "no". Aim for 2-3 sentences per answer.

PART 1 — Practice
Timer: 2 minutes — Answer all questions

Interview Questions

Answer each question in turn. Speak in full sentences. The timer starts automatically.

  1. Where did you go for your last holiday?
  2. Do you prefer going to the beach or the mountains? Why?
  3. How do you usually travel when you go on holiday?
  4. What's the most interesting place you've ever visited?
  5. Where would you like to go on holiday in the future?
  6. Do you prefer travelling with your family or with friends?
Examiner tip

Don't memorise answers. The examiner can tell. Speak naturally and show your personality.

PART 1 — Model Answers

Sample Answers

Click each button to reveal a model answer. Try to say your answer first!

Model Answer: Last summer, I went to Spain with my family. We stayed in a hotel near the beach and spent most of the time swimming and sunbathing. On the last day, we visited an old town and tried some local food. It was a wonderful experience because the weather was perfect and the people were very friendly.
Model Answer: I definitely prefer going to the beach because I love swimming and relaxing in the sun. The mountains are beautiful too, but I find them a bit boring because there isn't much to do except walking. At the beach, you can swim, play volleyball, or just lie down and read a book.
Model Answer: When I go on holiday with my family, we usually travel by car because it's more convenient for carrying luggage and stopping whenever we want. However, if the destination is far away, we fly. I prefer flying because it's faster, but I know it's worse for the environment.
Model Answer: The most interesting place I've ever visited is Istanbul in Turkey. It's an amazing city because it has a mix of European and Asian culture. We visited the Grand Bazaar, which is one of the largest markets in the world, and the Blue Mosque. The food was incredible too.
Model Answer: In the future, I would love to visit Japan because I'm very interested in Japanese culture. I'd love to see the cherry blossoms in Tokyo, try authentic sushi, and visit the temples in Kyoto. I think it would be fascinating to experience how different daily life is compared to Europe.
Model Answer: I prefer travelling with my family when I'm younger because they organize everything and I feel safe. However, as a teenager, I think travelling with friends is more fun because we have similar interests and can do more exciting activities. I enjoy both, but in different ways.
PART 2 — Long Turn

Part 2: Long Turn

Each candidate speaks for 1 minute about two photographs. Then the other candidate answers a short question.

Candidate A

Looks at two photos for 1 minute

Compares them and answers a question

Speaks for 1 minute

Candidate B

Listens to Candidate A

Answers a short question about the photos

Speaks for 30 seconds

Key skill

Compare the photos — don't just describe them. Use language of similarity and difference: "Both photos show...", "In the first photo... whereas in the second..."

PART 2 — Practice A
Timer: 1 minute — Compare the photos

Long Turn: Set A

Candidate A: Look at these two photographs. They show different types of holiday accommodation. Compare the photographs and say which type of accommodation you think is better for families.

Photo A: Waiting at an airport terminal with luggage

Photo B: A tropical beach resort with swimming pool

Follow-up (Candidate B)

Which type of travel experience do you prefer? (30 seconds)

PART 2 — Model Answer A

Long Turn Model Answer

Model Answer: Both photos show people on holiday, but they show very different types of travel experience. In the first photo, there are passengers at an airport terminal waiting with their luggage, probably on their way to a destination. The atmosphere seems busy and perhaps a bit stressful. In the second photo, there's a beautiful beach resort with a swimming pool, and people are relaxing in the sun. I think the beach resort is better for families because it's safe — children can swim in the pool and play on the sand, and parents can relax without worrying. However, the airport photo represents adventure and exploring new places, which is also exciting but more suitable for older people.
Follow-up Answer: I definitely prefer a beach resort because it allows me to completely switch off from school and relax. I love swimming and enjoying good food without having to think about planning activities or moving around. Airports and travelling can be quite stressful, so I prefer to stay in one place.
PART 2 — Practice B
Timer: 1 minute — Compare the photos

Long Turn: Set B

Candidate B: Look at these two photographs. They show different ways of exploring a city. Compare the photographs and say how you think the tourists are feeling in each photo.

Photo A: Tourists hiking in the mountains with backpacks

Photo B: Tourists sightseeing in a city with a guide and map

Follow-up (Candidate A)

How do you prefer to explore a new city? (30 seconds)

PART 2 — Model Answer B

Long Turn Model Answer

Model Answer: Both photos show tourists experiencing a new place, but in completely different ways. In the first photo, there are hikers in the mountains wearing backpacks and outdoor clothes. They look tired but happy — I think they feel a sense of achievement because they've been walking for a long time and are enjoying nature. In the second photo, there are tourists in a city looking at a map with a guide. They seem curious and maybe a bit lost, but excited about discovering new things. I believe the hikers are feeling peaceful and connected to nature, while the city tourists are feeling stimulated and curious about the culture.
Follow-up Answer: I prefer exploring a city with a guide or a map because I love learning about the history and culture of a place. Walking around freely allows you to discover hidden spots and take your time, whereas hiking in the mountains is physically demanding and you can't see as many sights in one day.
PART 3 — Collaborative Task

Part 3: Collaborative Task

Candidates discuss a situation together and make a decision. The examiner gives a question with five prompts.

Format

Phase 1: 2 minutes — Discuss all five prompts together

Phase 2: 1 minute — Make a final decision together

Skills tested:

  • Exchanging ideas and opinions
  • Agreeing and disagreeing politely
  • Suggesting and speculating
  • Negotiating and reaching a decision
  • Justifying your choices

This is a discussion — talk TO each other, not to the examiner. Build on what your partner says.

PART 3 — Practice A
Timer: 2 minutes — Discuss all options

Collaborative Task A

A group of students is planning a school trip abroad. Here are some travel options and a question for you to discuss. First, talk to each other about the advantages and disadvantages of each option. Then decide which trip the students should choose.

Discuss these options
  • ✈️ A city break in Paris visiting museums and landmarks
  • 🏖️ A beach holiday in Spain with water sports
  • 🥾 A hiking adventure in the Swiss Alps
  • 🎢 A theme park visit in Germany
  • 🏛️ A cultural exchange programme in London

Phase 2 (after timer): Decide together which trip would be best for the whole group.

PART 3 — Decision
Timer: 1 minute — Make your final decision

Make Your Decision

Now decide together: which one trip should the students choose? Be ready to explain your reasons and say why the other options are less suitable.

Useful language

"I think we should choose... because..."

"That's a good point, but I feel that..."

"How about we go with...?"

"I agree with you. Let's decide on..."

PART 3 — Model Answer A

Collaborative Task Model Answer

Model Discussion:

A: I think the Paris city break would be amazing because students can visit the Louvre and the Eiffel Tower — it's educational AND fun. What do you think?

B: That's a good point, but I feel that a beach holiday in Spain would be more relaxing for students. After a long school year, they deserve to rest and have fun, not walk around museums all day.

A: I see what you mean, however, a cultural exchange in London would be incredible because students could practise English with native speakers. That's more useful than just lying on a beach.

B: Yes, you're right about the English practice. But a theme park in Germany would be the most exciting option — everyone would love the roller coasters.

A: I agree that the theme park is exciting, but I think the cultural exchange in London would be more memorable and educational. Let's decide on that one.

B: I agree with you. The London cultural exchange is the best choice.
Decision: We decided that the school should choose the cultural exchange programme in London because it combines education with fun. Students can practise their English with native speakers, visit famous landmarks, and experience British culture. It's also a safe destination for school trips.
PART 4 — Discussion

Part 4: Extended Discussion

The examiner asks further questions related to the Part 3 topic. Candidates discuss together and with the examiner.

Format

Duration: 4-5 minutes

Focus: Expressing and justifying opinions, agreeing/disagreeing, speculating about broader themes

Skills tested:

  • Discussing abstract and social issues
  • Expressing opinions with reasons and examples
  • Responding to and challenging partner's ideas
  • Speculating about the future
  • Using a range of vocabulary and structures

This is the highest-level part. Show you can discuss ideas, not just facts. Use phrases like "I think the main issue is...", "In my country...", "I suppose it depends on..."

PART 4 — Practice
Timer: 3 minutes — Discuss all questions

Extended Discussion

Discuss these questions together. Give your opinions and reasons. The timer starts automatically.

  1. Do you think it's better to travel abroad or explore your own country? Why?
  2. How has technology changed the way people plan and book holidays?
  3. Is tourism always good for a country? What are the negative effects?
  4. Do you think young people should travel alone before they turn 18?
  5. What do you think holidays will look like in 50 years?
Top tip

Extend your answers with examples: "For example, in my school...", "I read that...", "My friend told me that..."

PART 4 — Model Answers

Discussion Model Answers

Model Answer: I think both have their advantages. Travelling abroad is great because you experience a completely different culture and language, which broadens your horizons. However, exploring your own country is also valuable because you learn about its history and geography. In my country, there are many beautiful places that tourists visit, but I've never been to them myself. So I think it's important to do both.
Model Answer: Technology has completely changed how people plan holidays. In the past, you had to go to a travel agency and rely on their advice. Now, you can research everything online — read reviews, compare prices, and book flights and hotels in minutes. Apps like Google Maps also make it much easier to navigate in a foreign country without getting lost.
Model Answer: No, tourism isn't always good. While it brings money to local areas, it can also cause problems. For example, in some cities, there are so many tourists that local people can't afford to live there anymore because prices go up. Also, too much tourism can damage natural areas and create pollution. I think it's important to be a responsible tourist and respect the places we visit.
Model Answer: I think it depends on the person and the destination. Some 17-year-olds are very mature and responsible, so they might be fine travelling alone to a safe European city. However, I believe it's better to travel with a group of friends or an adult until you're older. There are too many risks, and it's easy to get into trouble if you don't know what you're doing.
Model Answer: I think holidays in 50 years will be very different. We might be able to take short trips to space, which would be incredible! Also, because of climate change, some destinations might not exist anymore — for example, islands that are underwater. I imagine holidays will be more sustainable, and people might prefer to explore virtual reality destinations instead of travelling long distances.

Assessment Criteria

Candidates are marked on four areas, each scored 0-5:

Grammar & Vocabulary

Range and accuracy of grammar. Variety and appropriacy of vocabulary. Can you use complex structures?

Discourse Management

Can you organise your ideas clearly? Use linking words? Keep going without long pauses?

Pronunciation

Clear and understandable? Correct stress and intonation? Individual sounds accurate?

Interactive Communication

Do you respond to your partner? Start and maintain conversation? Ask for opinions?
