Food & Cooking

Practice Part 1 interview, Part 2 photo comparison, Part 3 collaborative task, and extra homework.

Part 1 — Interview

Part 1 — Interview

Answer these questions. Spend about 20 seconds on each.

• Do you like cooking?
I would say I enjoy cooking, but only when I have enough time. During the week I usually cook simple pasta, but at the weekend I like trying new recipes.
• What is your favourite dish?
My favourite dish is probably homemade pizza because you can put anything you like on it. I especially love it with fresh basil and mozzarella.
• How often do you eat out?
I eat out about once a week, usually with friends on a Friday evening. It's a nice way to relax, but it can be expensive.
• Do you prefer sweet or savoury food?
I definitely prefer savoury food. I like sweet things occasionally, but I would always choose a good sandwich or pizza over a cake.
• Have you ever tried cooking something from another country?
Yes, I recently tried making Thai curry from scratch. It was more difficult than I expected, but the fresh ingredients made it taste amazing.
Useful phrases:
I would say that...From my point of view...That's a tricky question because...On the one hand... on the other hand...I tend to...It depends on the situation.
Part 2 — Long Turn

Part 2 — Photo Comparison

Talk for 1 minute. Compare the photos and answer the question.

Why do you think some people prefer cooking at home to eating in restaurants?
Photo A:
A busy restaurant with elegant lighting and a waiter serving food
Photo B:
A person preparing a fresh meal in a cosy kitchen
In photo A, people are eating in a restaurant which looks convenient and special, perhaps for a celebration. In photo B, someone is cooking at home in a relaxed environment. I think many people prefer home cooking because it is cheaper and healthier, and they can control the ingredients. Restaurants are nice for treats or social occasions, but cooking at home gives you a sense of achievement. Also, sharing a homemade meal with family can feel more personal than a restaurant dinner.
Part 3 — Discussion

Part 3 — Collaborative Task

Discuss with your partner for 2–3 minutes, then decide.

A school wants to encourage healthier eating habits. Discuss the ideas and decide which two would work best.
  • Cooking classes for all students
  • Only healthy food in the cafeteria
  • Free fruit at break time
  • Information campaigns about sugar
  • Gardening club to grow vegetables
A: I think cooking classes would be useful because students learn skills they can use for life.
B: That's true, but banning unhealthy food might make students rebel and buy junk food outside school.
B: What about free fruit? It's simple and gives everyone a healthy option.
A: I agree, and a gardening club could also teach students where food comes from, which might make them eat more vegetables.
B: So let's choose cooking classes and the gardening club.
A: Yes, I agree with you.
Part 4 — Extension

Part 4 — Follow-up Discussion

Answer these deeper questions.

• Should schools teach cooking as a compulsory subject?
I think cooking should be taught in schools because it is a life skill. However, I would not make it compulsory at exam level; it could be a practical workshop instead.
• Is fast food always bad for you?
Fast food is not always bad if you choose wisely. Salads or grilled options can be healthy, but burgers and fried food eaten every day are definitely harmful.
• How important is it to eat meals together as a family?
Eating together is very important because it strengthens family bonds. It also encourages slower, healthier eating habits.
• What food trends are popular in your country?
In my country, plant-based and gluten-free options are becoming popular. People are also more interested in local, organic ingredients.
Vocabulary

Vocabulary & Phrases

cuisine — a style of cooking from a particular country or region

ingredient — one of the foods that you use to make a dish

appetising — looking or smelling so good that you want to eat it

from scratch — made using basic ingredients rather than a prepared mixture

balanced diet — a diet that includes the right amounts of different types of food

Homework

Reading, Listening & Writing

Reading

Listening

Writing
